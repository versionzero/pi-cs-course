The Computer Part

This project is will help enhance a Computer Science class I teach at the University of Lethbridge in the First Nations' Transition Program.

My intention is to use the proceeds of this project to purchase a computer for each student in the class. The computers will replace the need for textbooks, and allow for the students to work remotely when it is required. I have decided to use The Raspberry Pi as a platform for introducing basic Computer Science theory and practice. The are a variety of reasons for this, but most central is the size, price and the ability to use it at home on a regular TV.

The Eduction Part

The First Nations' Transition Program provides a transitional as well as supported year for qualifying FN students.

The transition program, loosely speaking, provides new students with an introduction to Western Ways of Knowing, including University-level educational expectations and how these ideas can be reconciled, merged or improved upon with Indigenous Ways of Knowing.

According to the Discover the University of Lethbridge Site, "The [FNTP] assists incoming students, or students returning to university education after an absence, in making a smooth transition into life at the University of Lethbridge. By connecting Aboriginal and university cultures, this program aims to increase access to post-secondary education and completion rates for First Nations' students."
